The Story So Far

Jensen Huang just delivered one of the densest GTC keynotes in years, and the headline isn’t any single chip — it’s that NVIDIA is methodically filling every gap in the AI stack. The Vera Rubin platform (Vera CPU + Rubin GPU, 3nm, HBM4, 10x perf/watt over Grace Blackwell) is the expected generational leap. What’s not expected: bolting Groq’s LPUs onto Rubin racks for a claimed 35x tokens-per-watt improvement on decode. If that number holds in production, it retroactively justifies the $20B acquisition and makes every other inference play look underpowered.

The software side is equally aggressive. NemoClaw gives enterprises an open-source agent stack that runs on DGX Spark at a desk. The Nemotron Coalition corrals Mistral, Perplexity, Cursor and five other labs into building open frontier models on NVIDIA’s infrastructure — a play to make “open” AI synonymous with NVIDIA compute. The Vera CPU’s 88 ARM cores and the N1/N1X laptop chips with MediaTek signal NVIDIA is serious about owning the CPU side too, not just accelerators.

The showmanship was peak Jensen: a Disney Olaf robot waddling onstage on NVIDIA’s physics engine, “the ChatGPT moment of self-driving” declared with four new robotaxi partners and an Uber deal, and — most audaciously — chips designed for orbital data centers. The $1 trillion order pipeline through 2027 (double the prior estimate) is the number Wall Street wanted. NVDA closed up ~2%. Whether the Feynman roadmap and space ambitions are vision or vaporware remains to be seen, but the near-term hardware is real and shipping.

2:00 PM PT

NVDA stock up ~2%; N1/N1X ARM laptop chips confirmed

Post-keynote, NVIDIA stock climbed roughly 2% as analysts digested the $1T order pipeline. Separately, details firmed up on the N1 and N1X — ARM-based laptop SoCs built with MediaTek, featuring the same core as DGX Spark’s GB10 and integrated graphics at RTX 5070 levels. Dell and Lenovo have at least eight models in the pipeline spanning XPS, Alienware, IdeaPad and Legion lines, shipping first half of 2026.

Vera Rubin Space-1: AI chips for orbital data centers

NVIDIA announced compute modules engineered for space — the Space-1 Vera Rubin module delivers up to 25x the AI compute of H100 in a size-, weight-, and power-constrained package. Partners include Aetherflux, Axiom Space, Kepler Communications, Planet, Sophia Space and Starcloud. This is early-stage and speculative, but if satellite constellations need on-orbit inference (and they increasingly do), NVIDIA wants to be the silicon up there.

“The ChatGPT moment of self-driving has arrived”

Huang declared autonomous driving’s inflection point, announcing BYD, Hyundai, Nissan, Geely and Isuzu are building Level 4 vehicles on NVIDIA’s Drive Hyperion platform. Uber will integrate robotaxi-ready cars into its network. NVIDIA also unveiled Alpamayo 1.5, a 10B-parameter reasoning VLA model that teaches AVs to reason through edge cases rather than just pattern-match. 110 robots were on display at the conference — NVIDIA wants “physical AI” to be as central to its identity as GPUs.

DLSS 5: 3D-guided neural rendering

NVIDIA revealed DLSS 5, which combines traditional computer graphics with AI-driven neural rendering. The demo used Resident Evil Requiem to showcase the tech. This is an iterative but meaningful step — DLSS has been one of NVIDIA’s most successful consumer AI features, and version 5 pushes further into letting neural networks fill in what the rasterizer can’t.

12:15 PM PT

NemoClaw and the Nemotron Coalition: NVIDIA’s agentic AI play

NemoClaw is an open-source enterprise agent stack — install in one command, pair with DGX Spark or DGX Station, and deploy long-running autonomous agents locally with built-in security and privacy controls. Alongside it, the Nemotron Coalition brings together Mistral AI, Perplexity, Cursor, LangChain, Black Forest Labs, Reflection AI, Sarvam and Thinking Machines Lab to collaboratively build open frontier models across six families: Nemotron (language), Cosmos (world/vision), Isaac GR00T (robotics), Alpamayo (driving), BioNeMo (biology) and Earth-2 (climate).

11:45 AM PT

Groq 3 LPU: the “Mellanox moment” for inference

The $20B Groq acquisition gets its coming-out party. NVIDIA will pack 256 Groq 3 LPUs into new LPX racks connected via Spectrum-X to Vera Rubin NVL72 systems — GPUs handle compute-heavy prefill, LPUs handle bandwidth-heavy token decode at 150 TB/s memory bandwidth (7x faster than Rubin alone). Huang claims 35x tokens-per-watt improvement and explicitly compared this to the Mellanox acquisition that transformed NVIDIA’s networking story. Groq 3 chips ship Q3 2026.

SiliconANGLE The Register
11:30 AM PT

Vera Rubin Platform unveiled: 10x perf/watt, 88 ARM cores

The main event: NVIDIA’s next-gen Vera Rubin platform combines the Rubin GPU (3nm, HBM4) with the Vera CPU — 88 custom “Olympus” ARM cores with SMT and confidential computing, NVIDIA’s first CPU built specifically for agentic AI and reinforcement learning. Seven chips are in full production. The 1.3-million-component system delivers 10x performance per watt over Grace Blackwell. Samples ship to tier-one cloud providers late 2026, full production early 2027. Vera will also be sold standalone to compete with Intel and AMD.

11:15 AM PT

$1 trillion in orders through 2027; CUDA turns 20; Feynman roadmap

Huang opened with a staggering number: combined Blackwell and Vera Rubin purchase orders are projected at $1 trillion through 2027 — double the prior $500B estimate. He marked CUDA’s 20th anniversary, crediting the software platform as the foundation of NVIDIA’s AI dominance. And he teased the post-Rubin roadmap: Feynman architecture with a new CPU called Rosa (named for Rosalind Franklin). The trillion-dollar pipeline is the real signal — demand for AI compute isn’t slowing.
