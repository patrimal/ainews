SoftBank secures $40 billion loan to deepen OpenAI bet

Today Bloomberg / Reuters / U.S. News

SoftBank closed a $40 billion unsecured bridge facility — arranged by JPMorgan, Goldman Sachs, Mizuho, SMBC, and MUFG — to fund further investment in OpenAI and general corporate purposes. The loan matures March 2027. SoftBank has previously committed $30 billion to OpenAI through Vision Fund 2.

Bloomberg’s framing: this is the largest-ever loan by a single company to fund an AI bet. SoftBank is now leveraged to the tune of $70B+ on the thesis that OpenAI will be the dominant AI platform — a concentration of risk that dwarfs the WeWork era.

Judge blocks Pentagon’s Anthropic blacklist, cites “First Amendment retaliation”

Yesterday CNBC / NPR / CNN

Federal Judge Rita Lin granted Anthropic’s preliminary injunction, barring the Trump administration from enforcing its supply-chain-risk designation or the directive banning federal use of Claude. Lin wrote that “nothing in the governing statute supports the Orwellian notion that an American company may be branded a potential adversary and saboteur of the U.S. for expressing disagreement with the government.” Implementation is delayed one week to allow a government appeal.

CNBC: a Cato Institute fellow said the opinion “reads as though the judge believes Anthropic is likely to succeed on the merits.” The First Amendment retaliation finding sets a precedent that could constrain the executive branch from using procurement blacklists to punish companies for public policy disagreements — a pattern that extends well beyond AI.

OpenAI shelves “adult mode” after mental health advisers vote unanimously against it

Yesterday TechCrunch / WinBuzzer / Engadget

OpenAI has indefinitely shelved its planned erotic chatbot feature after all eight mental-health advisers voted unanimously against it, citing a 12% failure rate in age verification that could expose millions of minors to explicit content. The company also struggled to train models — previously designed to refuse erotic content — to generate it while still filtering illegal material. Sam Altman first announced “adult mode” in October 2025.

TechCrunch’s read: OpenAI actually empowered an advisory board and then listened when it said no — a rarity in tech governance. The 12% age-verification failure rate across ChatGPT’s 100M+ weekly under-18 users would have meant millions of minors with potential access. xAI’s Grok deepfake debacle served as a cautionary accelerant.

ChatGPT ads hit $100M annualized revenue in six weeks

Yesterday CNBC / The Information

OpenAI’s U.S. advertising pilot crossed $100 million in annualized revenue within six weeks of launch, with 600+ advertisers onboard. Only ~20% of eligible users see ads on any given day, leaving significant room to scale. Self-serve advertiser tools launch in April, with expansion to Canada, Australia, and New Zealand planned.

CNBC notes that OpenAI reported no impact on privacy-related trust metrics despite the ad rollout — but the strategic significance is larger: OpenAI is building an ad-supported revenue layer inside a tool people use for research, reasoning, and decision-making, which is a fundamentally different ad surface than search or social.

Google ships Gemini 3.1 Flash Live and lets you import ChatGPT/Claude history

Yesterday 9to5Google / Google Blog / Bloomberg

Google launched Gemini 3.1 Flash Live, an audio/voice model powering Search Live and Gemini Live with faster, more natural real-time conversations. All generated audio includes SynthID watermarks. Separately, Google added “Import Memory” and “Import Chat History” tools that let free and paid users upload zipped conversation exports from ChatGPT or Claude — up to five .zip files (5GB each) per day.

9to5Google: the chat import tool is Google’s most aggressive user-acquisition play yet — reducing the switching cost from rival AI assistants to near zero. The SynthID watermarking on all Flash Live audio is notable as the first major deployment of provenance tracking on real-time AI voice output.

ARC-AGI-3: every frontier model scores under 1%, humans solve it effortlessly

Mar 24 ARC Prize / The Decoder / The Neuron

The ARC Prize Foundation released ARC-AGI-3, shifting from static puzzles to interactive, turn-based environments on a 64×64 color grid. Agents are dropped in with no instructions — no goal, no rules — and must figure out everything from scratch. Gemini 3.1 Pro topped at 0.37%, GPT-5.4 hit 0.26%, Opus 4.6 scored 0.25%, and Grok-4.20 scored 0.00%. Untrained humans solve these environments at 100%. A $2M prize pool is split across ARC-AGI-3 and final-year ARC-AGI-2 tracks on Kaggle.

The Decoder: ARC-AGI-3 is the only unsaturated general agentic intelligence benchmark as of March 2026. The near-zero scores expose a fundamental gap — current models can match or exceed humans on knowledge-heavy tasks but collapse when dropped into novel environments that require real-time exploration and rule inference, the kind of adaptive reasoning that defines general intelligence.
