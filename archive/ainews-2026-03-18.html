Today OpenAI / Dataconomy / Digital Trends

“Hunter Alpha” — mystery 1-trillion-parameter model fuels DeepSeek V4 speculation

Today Technology.org / Geeky Gadgets / Seeking Alpha

An anonymous 1-trillion-parameter model called Hunter Alpha appeared on OpenRouter with free access, reasoning capabilities, and a 1M-token context window. It describes itself as “a Chinese AI model primarily trained in Chinese” with a May 2025 training cutoff — identical to DeepSeek’s chatbot. By Sunday it had processed over 160 billion tokens. Neither DeepSeek nor OpenRouter will comment.

Technology.org notes the same anonymous provider previously released “Pony Alpha,” which turned out to be Zhipu AI’s GLM-5. The architectural fingerprint could point to DeepSeek V4 or Zhipu’s GLM-6 — either way, someone is stealth-testing a frontier model and letting the internet do free QA.

Mistral launches Forge — enterprise “build your own AI” platform — plus Small 4 hybrid model

Yesterday TechCrunch / VentureBeat / Silicon Republic

Announced at GTC, Mistral Forge lets enterprises build custom frontier-grade models trained on their own data using Mistral’s open-weight library. Early partners include Ericsson, the European Space Agency, and Singapore’s DSO. Alongside it, Mistral Small 4 ships as a 119B-parameter hybrid model (6B active per token) that auto-switches between Magistral (reasoning), Pixtral (multimodal), and Devstral (coding), delivering 40% faster end-to-end completion and 3x more requests per second vs. Small 3.

TechCrunch’s framing: Mistral is betting that enterprises want to own their AI, not rent it — a direct challenge to OpenAI and Anthropic’s API-consumption model. The forward-deployed engineering team (borrowed from Palantir’s playbook) signals this is a services play, not just a platform.

World launches AgentKit — iris-scan “proof of human” for AI shopping agents

Yesterday TechCrunch / CoinDesk / PYMNTS

Sam Altman’s World (formerly Worldcoin) released AgentKit, a developer toolkit that lets AI agents carry cryptographic proof they’re backed by a real human via World ID iris scans. It integrates with Coinbase’s x402 protocol for agent-to-agent transactions. Merchants can request one-time human-approval signals when an AI agent tries to checkout, apply coupons, or complete purchases.

McKinsey projects agentic commerce could hit $3–5 trillion globally by 2030; Bain estimates AI agents could handle 25% of U.S. e-commerce. TechCrunch notes this tackles a real problem — without proof-of-human checks, agent commerce is wide open to bot abuse, coupon fraud, and synthetic-identity attacks.
