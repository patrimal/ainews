Anthropic’s “Claude Mythos” leaked via unsecured data store — a new model tier above Opus

Yesterday Fortune / The Decoder / Seeking Alpha

Fortune discovered ~3,000 unpublished assets on Anthropic’s servers, including draft documentation for “Claude Mythos” (internal codename “Capybara”). Anthropic confirmed the model exists and called it “the most capable we’ve built to date” — a new tier above Opus with “dramatically higher scores” in coding, academic reasoning, and cybersecurity. Internal docs warn it’s “currently far ahead of any other AI model in cyber capabilities” and could enable large-scale vulnerability exploitation. Anthropic blamed “human error” in CMS configuration.

Fortune’s reporting: Anthropic’s own leaked safety assessment says the model “presages an upcoming wave of models that can exploit vulnerabilities in ways that far outpace the efforts of defenders” — the first time a frontier lab’s internal documents have revealed this level of concern about their own model’s offensive cyber capabilities.

OpenAI kills Sora — downloads fell 75%, Disney exits $1B deal

Mar 24 TechCrunch / Variety / NBC News

OpenAI is shutting down the Sora video app, which launched in October 2025 as a TikTok-style AI video platform. Downloads plummeted ~75% from the November peak (3.3M to 1.1M in February), and the app made just $2.1M lifetime from in-app purchases. Disney has exited a three-year partnership that would have given Sora access to 200+ Disney/Marvel/Pixar/Star Wars characters and included a planned $1B investment in OpenAI. The company is reportedly redirecting Sora’s compute to robotics research.

TechCrunch: the “cameo” deepfake feature that was Sora’s differentiator turned out to be exactly what repelled users. The Disney pullout — including a $1B planned investment — signals that even media partners who signed on enthusiastically saw the product-market fit collapse in real time.

OpenAI offers PE firms 17.5% guaranteed returns for $4B enterprise joint venture

Mar 26 Reuters / Sherwood News / Unite.AI

OpenAI is negotiating with TPG, Advent International, Bain Capital, and Brookfield for a $4B joint venture valued at $10B pre-money. The deal includes preferred equity with a guaranteed 17.5% minimum return — unlike Anthropic’s rival $1B offer, which doesn’t guarantee returns. The strategic play: PE firms’ hundreds of portfolio companies become a captive enterprise sales channel for OpenAI products, bypassing the slower deal-by-deal sales cycle.

Reuters: the 17.5% guarantee is financially aggressive for a company projecting $14B in 2026 losses — but it buys something money alone can’t: instant distribution across hundreds of enterprise companies via PE portfolios, a channel Anthropic is also pursuing but at one-quarter the scale.

Shopify Agentic Storefronts go live — millions of merchants sell inside ChatGPT, Gemini, Copilot

Mar 24 Shopify / Modern Retail / CNBC

Every eligible Shopify merchant’s products are now discoverable inside ChatGPT, Google AI Mode, Microsoft Copilot, and the Gemini app — no separate integration, no extra apps, no transaction fees beyond standard processing. Purchases complete on merchant storefronts, not inside the AI conversation. A new “Agentic” plan lets brands not on Shopify add products to the catalog for AI channel distribution.

Modern Retail: unlike OpenAI’s abandoned Instant Checkout, Shopify’s approach keeps merchants as the merchant of record with full control of customer relationships — which is why it actually shipped to millions of stores instead of dying in pilot. This is the infrastructure layer for AI-native commerce.
