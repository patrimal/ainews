OpenAI kills Sora: $1M/day burn, under 500K users, Disney partnership unwinding

Yesterday TechCrunch / WSJ / Bloomberg

TechCrunch’s deep dive (via a Wall Street Journal investigation) reveals the real numbers behind OpenAI’s Sora shutdown: the video generator peaked at ~1 million users, collapsed to under 500K, and was burning roughly $1 million per day in compute. The app shuts down April 26, the API in September. Disney’s $1 billion partnership — which had licensed Mickey Mouse and Cinderella for Sora — is also unwinding. Sam Altman’s call: kill the money pit, free up GPU capacity, refocus.

TechCrunch’s core argument: this wasn’t a data-grab conspiracy or an ethics retreat — Sora died the most mundane death possible. Video generation is too expensive and nobody was willing to pay for it. The first real unit-economics casualty of the AI scaling era.

Apple opens Siri to every AI rival in iOS 27 — Claude, Gemini, Grok, and anyone else who wants in

Mar 26 Bloomberg / Cult of Mac

Bloomberg reports Apple will open Siri to competing AI assistants via a new “Extensions” system in iOS 27.
