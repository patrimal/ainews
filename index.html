Anthropic v. Pentagon: injunction hearing today at 1:30 PM PST

Today Euronews / TechCrunch / Lawfare

Judge Rita Lin hears Anthropic’s motion for a preliminary injunction in San Francisco federal court this afternoon. Anthropic argues the Pentagon’s “supply chain risk” designation — historically reserved for foreign adversaries like Huawei — was unlawfully applied to a domestic company over a policy disagreement about military AI use. Microsoft filed an amicus brief supporting Anthropic, and 22 retired military officials called the designation “a misuse of government authority for retribution.”

Lawfare’s analysis: the government’s legal position has a fundamental structural problem — the statute (10 U.S.C. § 3252) was designed to exclude foreign adversaries from the supply chain, not to punish domestic companies for disagreeing on policy. If Judge Lin agrees, it sets a precedent limiting executive overreach against the entire AI sector.

Altman exits Helion board as OpenAI negotiates 5 GW fusion power deal

Today TechCrunch / Bloomberg

Sam Altman stepped down from Helion Energy’s board of directors to clear the way for OpenAI to negotiate a power purchase agreement with the fusion startup. OpenAI is in advanced talks to secure 5 gigawatts of Helion’s electricity by 2030, scaling to 50 GW by 2035. Altman, who personally invested in Helion, said he would recuse himself from deal negotiations but retains a financial interest.

TechCrunch’s framing: this is OpenAI betting its infrastructure future on fusion power that hasn’t been demonstrated at commercial scale yet. 5 GW by 2030 would power roughly five hyperscale data centers — but Helion has yet to achieve net energy gain in a single reactor, let alone build a grid-connected plant.

Musk unveils Terafab: $25B chip fab for Tesla, SpaceX, and xAI

Yesterday Bloomberg / TechCrunch / Engadget

Elon Musk announced “Terafab,” a joint Tesla/SpaceX/xAI semiconductor fabrication facility in Austin, Texas. The $25B project targets 2nm process technology and aims to produce inference chips for Tesla vehicles and Optimus robots alongside custom D3 chips for orbital AI satellites. The facility is planned for the North Campus of Giga Texas and would consolidate chip design, lithography, fabrication, packaging, and testing under one roof.

Electrek’s take: this “reeks of desperation” — Musk has never shipped a custom chip at scale, 2nm fab expertise doesn’t exist outside TSMC and Samsung, and the announcement comes as Tesla’s AI strategy faces growing Wall Street skepticism. The ambition is real; the timeline credulity gap is enormous.

Physical Intelligence: robots learn sub-millimeter precision in 15 minutes

Mar 19 Physical Intelligence / Humanoids Daily

Physical Intelligence (Pi) released RL Tokens (RLT), a reinforcement learning method that bolts onto existing Vision-Language-Action models and lets robots master contact-rich precision tasks — screwdriving, zip-tying, ethernet insertion — in minutes instead of days. RLT adds a single output token as a compressed bottleneck between the VLA and a lightweight actor-critic network trained directly on the robot with hundreds of updates per second.

The “last millimeter” problem has been the gap between impressive demos and actual deployment. Pi’s results show RLT-enhanced robots completing precise tasks up to 3× faster than the base model, and in ethernet insertion tests, faster than human teleoperation — a concrete benchmark for when robots stop needing human babysitting.

Microsoft scales back Copilot “bloat” across Windows 11

Mar 20 TechCrunch / IT Pro / Digital Trends

Microsoft announced it will reduce Copilot AI integrations in Photos, Widgets, Notepad, and Snipping Tool. EVP Pavan Davuluri wrote that the company is becoming “more intentional about how and where Copilot integrates across Windows” and will focus on experiences that are “genuinely useful.” Forced Copilot app installations have also been halted, and restructuring hit the Copilot division.

TechCrunch’s read: this is Microsoft acknowledging that cramming AI into every surface backfired. The rollback follows months of user pushback over privacy concerns and unwanted AI features. When your own EVP has to publicly commit to “genuinely useful,” you’ve admitted the previous standard was “indiscriminate.”
