Today CybersecurityNews / Artificial Lawyer / PCWorld

Meta abandons open source with Muse Spark — first model from Superintelligence Labs is fully proprietary

Apr 8 CNBC / Fortune / The Register

Meta unveiled Muse Spark, the debut model from its Meta Superintelligence Labs led by Alexandr Wang. It’s a natively multimodal reasoning model with tool use, visual chain-of-thought, and multi-agent orchestration — and it’s completely closed. No weights, no download, no open source. The model supports a “Thinking” mode for deeper reasoning and an “Instant” mode for speed, and will roll out across Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, Messenger, and Ray-Ban Meta glasses. Meta spent billions recruiting from OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google to staff the unit.

The Register’s headline says it all: “Meta’s new model is as open as Zuckerberg’s private school.” The company that positioned Llama as the anti-OpenAI open alternative just shipped a proprietary model behind closed APIs. The open-source brand is dead; the question is whether the developer community notices before the next Llama release.
