ARC-AGI-3 launches: first interactive reasoning benchmark for AI

Today ARC Prize / Y Combinator

François Chollet and the ARC Prize team launched ARC-AGI-3, a fundamentally new benchmark with 1,000+ levels across 150+ interactive, video-game-like environments. Unlike the static grid puzzles in ARC-AGI-1 and 2, version 3 requires AI agents to explore, plan, remember, and adapt in real time — testing capabilities like goal-directedness and multi-step learning that current models struggle with.

ARC Prize’s framing: this is the first benchmark that formally compares human vs. AI action efficiency (learning efficiency). Early preview data showed frontier models plateauing well below human performance, suggesting the “just scale it” approach hits a ceiling when static pattern-matching gives way to interactive reasoning.

Claude Code v2.1.83: managed settings, hook events, transcript search

Today Anthropic / Claude Code changelog

Claude Code shipped v2.1.83 with a batch of enterprise and power-user features: a managed-settings.d/ drop-in directory for deploying independent policy fragments, CwdChanged and FileChanged hook events for reactive environment management, transcript search with / and n/N navigation, and CLAUDE_CODE_SUBPROCESS_ENV_SCRUB=1 to strip credentials from subprocess environments. Agents can now declare initialPrompt in frontmatter to auto-submit their first turn.

The managed-settings drop-in directory is the significant one for teams: it lets IT deploy policy fragments independently without merge conflicts, which is the missing piece for enterprise rollouts where security, compliance, and dev teams each own different config slices.

Anthropic v. Pentagon: judge calls ban “an attempt to cripple,” ruling expected this week

Yesterday NPR / CNBC / The Hill

U.S. District Judge Rita Lin heard 90 minutes of oral argument and repeatedly pressed the government on why Anthropic was designated a supply chain risk. “I don’t know if it’s murder, but it looks like an attempt to cripple Anthropic,” Lin said. She called the Pentagon’s actions “troubling” because they weren’t “tailored to the stated national security concern” and questioned whether the government was punishing Anthropic for going public with its objections. No ruling was issued; Lin asked both sides to file additional evidence by Wednesday and indicated she’ll decide before end of week.

CNBC’s read: the judge’s skepticism was pointed and sustained — she told the government “that seems a pretty low bar” when they argued Anthropic’s policy disagreement justified blacklisting. If she grants the injunction, it would be the first court ruling limiting executive power to punish domestic AI companies over policy disputes.

iOS 26.4 ships today — without the promised AI-powered Siri

Yesterday Macworld / Apple Insider / 9to5Mac

Apple released iOS 26.4 with AI-powered Playlist Playground in Apple Music, new emoji, and accessibility improvements. Notably absent: the Gemini-powered Siri overhaul that Apple has been teasing since January, featuring on-screen context awareness, multi-step action chaining, and natural multi-turn conversation. That’s now pushed to iOS 26.5, with beta expected March 30.

Apple Insider’s take: the macOS Tahoe 26.4 release notes explicitly confirm “still no new Siri,” making this the third consecutive point release where Apple delayed the feature. The gap between Apple’s AI messaging and its shipping cadence keeps widening while competitors ship weekly.
