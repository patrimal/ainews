Karpathy’s “LLM Wiki” goes viral — 16M views, 5K GitHub stars, and a rethink of RAG

Apr 4 Andrej Karpathy / VentureBeat / Analytics India

Andrej Karpathy posted that he’s spending less time using LLMs to generate code and more time using them to “compile” raw research into persistent, interlinked markdown wikis. The pattern: dump papers, notes, and articles into a folder, point an LLM at it, and let it build and maintain a structured knowledge base with backlinks, categories, and encyclopedia-style articles. His personal wiki grew to 100 articles and 400K words on a single topic. He followed up with a GitHub gist “idea file” — not code, just the architecture — arguing that in the age of LLM agents, sharing the idea is more useful than sharing the app.

Karpathy’s core insight: at personal scale, structured markdown beats vector search, and LLMs handle interlinking better than humans. VentureBeat calls it a “bypass of RAG” — instead of retrieving on the fly, you pre-compile knowledge into a living document that compounds over time.
