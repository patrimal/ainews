Google drops Gemma 4 under Apache 2.0 — 31B model ranks #3 on open-weight leaderboards

Today Google AI Blog / Dataconomy

Google released the Gemma 4 family in four sizes (2B, 4B, 26B, 31B parameters) under the Apache 2.0 license. The flagship 31B model supports 256K context, processes text, audio, and video natively, and runs on edge devices including phones and Raspberry Pi with near-zero latency. Trained on 140+ languages with built-in support for complex reasoning, multi-step planning, and code generation.

Dataconomy’s take: a 31B model handling multimodal input with 256K context that runs locally on a phone and ships under Apache 2.0 moves the floor for what “open-weight” means in practice — smaller teams can now ship products that required API-tier models six months ago.

Netflix open-sources VOID — first public model does physics-aware object removal from video

Today Hugging Face / Netflix Research

Netflix quietly dropped VOID (Video Object and Interaction Deletion) on Hugging Face — their first-ever public model release. VOID doesn’t just erase objects from video; it simulates the physical consequences. Remove a person holding a guitar and the guitar falls. Built on CogVideoX with a novel “quadmask” conditioning system that separately encodes primary objects, overlap regions, physically affected regions, and background.

Physics-aware video inpainting at this quality is genuinely novel. The quadmask approach solves what prior inpainting models couldn’t: cascading physical effects when objects are removed from a scene. And Netflix releasing it open-weight suggests they see more value in the ecosystem than in keeping it proprietary.
