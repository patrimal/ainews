Generalist’s GEN-1 hits 99% reliability on physical manipulation — trained on human wearable data, not robots

Apr 11 Robotics & Automation News / SiliconANGLE / MLQ

San Mateo startup Generalist unveiled GEN-1, a physical AI foundation model achieving 99% success rates on production tasks — folding boxes, packing phones, servicing robot vacuums — up from 64% for previous models. Instead of expensive robot-generated datasets, GEN-1 was pretrained on data from low-cost wearable devices worn by humans. When disrupted mid-task, it improvises novel recovery strategies on the fly — not pre-programmed routines.

Robotics & Automation News: the 64% → 99% jump crosses the threshold from “impressive demo” to “deployable in production.” The human-wearable pretraining approach sidesteps robotics’ biggest bottleneck (expensive robot data collection) and the improvised recovery behavior puts GEN-1 in a fundamentally different category from prior systems.

Karpathy proposes “LLM Wiki” — a living knowledge layer that makes the case RAG is broken

Apr 9 Andrej Karpathy / GitHub

Karpathy published a GitHub Gist outlining “LLM Wiki” — a pattern where an LLM maintains a persistent, interlinked markdown knowledge base instead of retrieving raw documents on every query. When new sources arrive, the LLM summarizes them, links to existing articles, and writes updates back into the wiki. He praised “Farzapedia” as a real implementation: 2,500 personal entries compiled into 400 detailed, backlinked articles. His core argument: RAG never accumulates knowledge — every complex query requiring synthesis across multiple documents starts from scratch.

Karpathy’s argument: the knowledge artifact is explicit, navigable, and local — not implicit in some provider’s opaque memory system. You can see exactly what the AI does and doesn’t know. Multiple analyses are calling this a paradigm shift for personal knowledge management, replacing RAG’s “search every time” approach with structured, accumulated understanding.

AI Alliance launches Project Tapestry for federated open-model training — LeCun named Chief Science Advisor

Apr 7 HPCwire / AI Alliance / PR Newswire

The AI Alliance, a 200+ member nonprofit coalition, launched Project Tapestry — an open-source platform enabling distributed, federated training of frontier AI models. Institutions and nations contribute compute and data while retaining control and producing sovereign derivative models aligned to their own laws, culture, and values. The Alliance appointed Yann LeCun as Chief Science Advisor. LeCun, who left Meta in late 2025 to launch AMI Labs ($1.03B seed round), will guide the project from architecture into global implementation.

The sovereignty angle is the real story: Project Tapestry lets countries build frontier-class open models without surrendering data to US hyperscalers. LeCun’s involvement signals that the open-vs-closed AI debate is moving from ideology to infrastructure — with real money and geopolitics behind it.
