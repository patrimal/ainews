Super Micro co-founder arrested in $2.5B Nvidia GPU smuggling case

Yesterday Bloomberg / CNBC / Fortune

The DOJ unsealed “Operation Gatekeeper,” charging Super Micro co-founder Yih-Shyan “Wally” Liaw and two others with conspiring to smuggle $2.5 billion worth of Nvidia AI servers to China. The alleged scheme: purchase orders from a Southeast Asian front company, assembly and shipment to Supermicro’s Taiwan facility, then removal of identifying packaging before forwarding to Chinese customers. Each defendant faces up to 20 years. SMCI shares crashed 28% on Friday.

CNBC notes this is the highest-profile crackdown yet on AI chip export violations, involving a co-founder of a major Nasdaq-listed company, not some fly-by-night broker. The scale — $2.5 billion — suggests the chip embargo has a far bigger enforcement gap than Washington has publicly acknowledged.
