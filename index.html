PwC: 74% of AI’s economic value is going to just 20% of companies

Apr 13 PwC / IT Pro / ResultSense

PwC surveyed 1,217 senior executives across 25 sectors globally. The top-performing 20% of companies generate 7.2x more AI-driven revenue and efficiency gains than the average competitor. Meanwhile, 56% of companies report no significant financial benefit from AI at all. The single strongest predictor of AI success: industry convergence — using AI to expand beyond traditional sector boundaries — ahead of efficiency gains alone.

PwC’s data: “everyone is doing AI” has become meaningless. More than half of companies are spending on AI with nothing to show for it. The gap between AI winners and AI tourists is 7.2x and widening — this is becoming a winner-take-most market.

Nature: Human scientists still trounce AI agents by 55+ points on complex tasks

Apr 13 Nature / MIT Technology Review

A Nature report found that on the Humanity’s Last Exam benchmark — 2,500 expert-level questions across dozens of academic fields — the best AI models score roughly 35% while human domain experts average around 90%. That’s a 55+ point gap. Enterprise agentic AI systems separately show a 37% gap between lab benchmark scores and real-world deployment performance. The best models (Opus 4.6, Gemini 3.1 Pro) have crossed 50% on HLE, up from near-zero a year ago, but experts with PhDs still outperform them two-to-one.

Nature’s framing: the benchmark gap is a cold shower for anyone conflating rapid improvement with arrival. AI is getting better fast, but “doubled from 18% to 35%” and “matches human experts” are very different sentences. The 37% lab-to-deployment gap makes it worse.

Sora web app shuts down in 10 days — OpenAI’s most expensive product failure

Apr 26 OpenAI / Variety / The Decoder

OpenAI’s Sora web and app experience shuts down April 26, with the API following September 24. The text-to-video tool was reportedly costing $1M/day to operate. Active users dropped from 1 million to under 500,000. Disney pulled its planned $1 billion investment. Creators must export content at sora.chatgpt.com/exports/me before the deadline — OpenAI hasn’t confirmed a final export window after shutdown.

Variety’s reporting: Sora launched five months ago, burned through a billion-dollar partnership, and couldn’t sustain its own compute costs. This is the most expensive AI product failure to date — and a signal that “impressive demo” and “sustainable product” remain very different things.
