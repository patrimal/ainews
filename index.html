States pass wave of AI chatbot safety laws — Tennessee bans AI therapists, Georgia and Idaho follow

Today Transparency Coalition / WKRN

Tennessee Governor Bill Lee signed SB 1580, prohibiting AI systems from representing themselves as qualified mental health professionals — passed 32-0 in the Senate and 94-0 in the House. Georgia sent three AI bills to Governor Kemp this week: SB 540 (chatbot disclosure and child safety, passed House 114-0), SB 444 (banning insurance decisions based solely on AI), and SR 789 (AI study committee). Idaho sent four AI bills to the governor covering education, social media, chatbot safety, and synthetic media. Nebraska, South Carolina, Arizona, and California all have additional bills advancing.

Transparency Coalition’s analysis: this is the first week where multiple states signed AI chatbot safety bills simultaneously with near-unanimous bipartisan support. The emerging template — targeting deceptive AI identity claims rather than banning AI outright — is becoming the default state-level regulatory approach.

OpenAI kills Sora to redirect compute toward autonomous agents — Disney’s $1B deal dies with it

Yesterday Variety / Deadline / TechCrunch

Sam Altman explained on “Mostly Human” why OpenAI is shutting down Sora: the video app burned ~$1M/day in compute, peaked under 1M users, and the company needs to “concentrate compute and product capacity into automated researchers and companies.” Disney found out 30 minutes after their last joint meeting and has shelved its planned $1B OpenAI investment — a deal that would have put AI-generated Disney characters on Sora. The app closes April 26; the API follows September 24. Altman said he “felt terrible” telling Disney CEO Josh D’Amaro but called it a necessary resource allocation decision.

TechCrunch’s framing: this is OpenAI explicitly conceding that content generation lost the resource war to code agents and automated research. The strategic calculus is now public — autonomous tools that drive enterprise revenue beat consumer creative apps, no matter how flashy the demos.
