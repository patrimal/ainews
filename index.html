Reddit’s bot crackdown goes live: automated accounts get labels, suspicious users face “human verification”

Today TechCrunch / Engadget

Starting today, Reddit labels automated accounts with an [App] badge on their profiles and may prompt suspicious accounts to verify they’re human. Verification methods under consideration include passkeys, third-party biometric checks, and government ID services. CEO Steve Huffman emphasized the approach is “privacy-first” — confirming a person exists behind an account, not who they are. Reddit says it removes 100,000 bot accounts daily. Importantly, using AI to write posts isn’t banned; only fully automated accounts are targeted.

TechCrunch’s framing: this is the first major platform to draw a line between “AI-assisted human” and “fully automated bot,” a distinction that will likely become the industry template as every social platform grapples with the same problem.

Why OpenAI really killed Sora: $1M/day burn, 500K users, and Disney found out an hour before the public

Mar 29 TechCrunch / Slate

TechCrunch published its deep-dive into Sora’s demise on Saturday. The numbers are brutal: after launch, global users peaked around one million, then collapsed below 500,000 — while operational costs held steady at roughly $1 million per day. Slate’s analysis: Sora’s failure is the first major proof point that AI video generation doesn’t have viable unit economics at consumer scale — and OpenAI killing its most-hyped product signals the company is finally prioritizing revenue over spectacle.
